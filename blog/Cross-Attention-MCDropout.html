Cross-Attention
vs MC-Dropout

How multimodal medical AI learns to decide what to trust under uncertainty.

Imagine a multidisciplinary medical board evaluating a stroke patient. Each specialist analyzes a different imaging modality: NCCT, CTA, and perfusion maps.

The real challenge in multimodal AI is not prediction — it is deciding which modality to trust when data becomes noisy or incomplete.

The core problem is not accuracy, but reliability under uncertainty.

Cross-Attention and MC-Dropout represent two fundamentally different solutions to this problem.

Cross-Attention learns global statistical relationships between modalities. Once trained, it applies fixed weights regardless of input quality.

This makes it powerful but rigid: noisy modalities may still be over-trusted.

Key idea

UNCERTAINTY

MC-Dropout introduces a different philosophy: uncertainty estimation at inference time.

By running multiple stochastic passes, the model estimates variance per modality.

High variance = low confidence. Low variance = reliable signal.

Each modality adapts its influence based on its confidence.

This transforms multimodal fusion into a patient-specific decision system instead of a fixed global rule.

In medical AI, where noise is unavoidable, this difference becomes critical.
